Cardfight!! Vanguard is a card game that players create their own decks from cards they have collected to battle with one another!
Power up your avatar and work together with your allied units, in a showdown against your opponent's deck!
Enjoy the exciting battle where you can turn the tides with the Trigger you draw!

Vanguard Cards

Normal Unit

The Basic Unit. The banner at the bottom of the card is black.

Trigger Unit

The banner at the bottom is yellow. Activate various effects when it is revealed from your deck!

Order Card

“Order” means a wide variety of commands such as operations, magic, and the use of items.

Preparation for Cardfight
Once you've learned about the cards and the playmat, let's prepare for the cardfight using your Deck!
  1. Put the markers and tickets face down and ensure it does not mix with your main deck.
  2. Choose 1 grade 0 card from the deck of 50 cards, and place it on the vanguard circle face down.
  3. Shuffle the remaining 49 cards and place them here.
  4. Determine who goes first randomly with rock paper scissors or roll a dice for the highest.
  5. Draw 5 cards from the top of the deck without showing your opponent to form your hand.
  6. Just once, you may return any number of cards your hand to the bottom of your deck, and draw the same number of cards you returned from the top of your deck. Then, shuffle your deck.
Point

It is advantageous to have grades 1, 2, and 3 cards in your hand. Grade 0, or more than 2 cards of the same grade are recommended to be returned to the deck!

Let the Cardfight Begin!

When both players are ready, it's time to start the fight!

Turn your vanguard face up together with your opponent at the same time by saying "Stand up, Vanguard!" together. The turn starts as soon as the order of play is decided!

Stand & Draw Phases

Each player's turn begins with "Stand" and "Draw" phases. If there are any horizontal cards on the players field (rest), turn them vertial (stand), and draw 1 card from the top of your deck. If there are no cards to stand, drawing 1 card from the top of your deck would be fine.

Ride Phase

You can power up your vanguard once per turn. This called "Ride". Choose a card from your hand that is grade+1 to the vanguard and stack it face up on your vanguard. Your card's power is increased as well!

Call Phase

Summon allies to fight together with your vanguard by "Calling" them to the rear-guard circle. You may call as many units as you want in a turn, but the unit's grade has to be less than or equal to the grade of your vanguard. During this phase, you may also activate 1 Normal Order from your hand.
Example: You may call units of grades 2, 1, or 0, when your vanguard is of grade 2.

Attack Step

After powering up your vanguard by "Riding" it and "Calling" allies to your rear-guard circles, it is time to "Attack your opponent's Vanguard." The attacks occur for 1 card at a time.

  1. First, choose your attacking unit and turn it horizontal to show that the unit has attempted and attack. Only your vanguard and rear-guard units on the front row may attack.
  2. If you have a unit behind the attacking unit with "Boost", you may turn it horizontal as well to add its attack power to the attacking unit.
  3. Choose an opponent's unit to attack. You may only attack units on the front row of your opponent's field. (However, the player who starts first may not attack on his or her first turn.)
Drive Check (when a Vanguard attacks!)

When a vanguard attacks, you have a chance to "Drive Check"! Put 1 card from the top of your deck face up on the trigger zone. If it is a trigger unit with an icon on the top right corner of the card, an effect will occur. Add the card from the drive check to your hand!!!

Power Calculation

Compare the power of the unit that attacked, and the unit that it is being attacked. If the attacking unit's power is higher than or equal to the unit being attacked, the attack will "Hit". If the attack hits a rear-guard, the rear-guard unit is put into the drop zone. If the attack hits a vanguard, "Damage Check" occurs!

Guard Step

The player being attacked may be able to defend the attack. Place a card from your hand onto the "Guardian Circle" horizontally to add the value of the "Shield" to the power of the unit being attacked. You may place as many cards as you want onto the guardian circle from hand!
Additionally, you may play 1 Blitz Order Card during this step!

The power up during guard step is only effective for 1 attack. When that attack ends, the power of the defending unit returns to its original value and the cards on the guardian circle go to the drop zone.

Damage Check

When the attack hits an opponent's vanguard, the opponent puts 1 card from the top of his or her deck face up into the trigger zone. That card will go to the damage zone face up, but if it is a trigger unit, its effect will occur. After performing damage check, if there are still units that can attack, return to Attack Step.

As you continue making hits on your opponent's vanguard, when the cards in your opponent's damage zone reaches 6, you win!
State of the Card

There are 2 possible states for cards placed on the playmat.

Special Abilities (Keyword Abilities)

Cards can have all sorts of abilities, but there are special abilities called keyword abilities, that have similar effects and activation conditions.
Example: Sentinel - An ability that protects you from your opponent's attack when guarding. Follow what each Sentinel does to up your defenses (You may only have up to four cards with "[CONT]:Sentinel" in a deck.)

Types of Triggers

There are 4 different types of triggers. If these icons are revealed during a drive or damage check, they provide various effects until the end of turn.

  • Critical Trigger: Choose 1 unit and it get Power +10000. Then choose another unit (you may choose the same unit), and when this unit's attack hits a vanguard, you may increase the damage by 1 card.

  • Draw Trigger: Choose 1 unit, and it gets Power +10000. Then, draw 1 card from the top of your deck, and add it to your hand.

  • Front Trigger: All of your front row units get Power +10000. This trigger is only available to Accel Type clans.

  • Heal Trigger: Choose 1 unit, and it gets Power +10000. Then, compare the number of cards in both you and your opponent's damage zone. If you have more cards or the same number of cards as your opponent, you can move 1 card from your damage zone to your drop zone.

Types of Skill Icons

All units have 1 of the following "Skill Icons". On the top left corner of the card, depending on the grade, there is one of the following skills: "Boost", "Intercept", "Twin Drive". There are also units with the "Gift Icon". The list below are the skills and the grade normally associated with.

  • Boost (Grade 0, Grade 1): When a unit in the front row attacks, rest this unit to add its power to the attacking unit in the same column.

  • Intercept (Grade 2): Units with this icon on a rear-guard circle of your front row can move to the guardian circle during the guard step of your opponent's turn to defend.

  • Twin Drive (Grade 3): When attacking with the vanguard, you must drive check 2 times.

  • Gift Icon: If you ride this unit, you can get one of the gift markers from the 3 types supported.

Card Abilities & Types of Icons

Every card's individual ability, effect; condition for activation, and cost are written in the translucent box in the middle part of the card.
Once you get used to cardfight, try reading this portion and fulfilling the conditions to activate the abilities!

At the beginning of every ablity, 1 of the following activation timing icons is shown. The place where the ability can be used, displayed in [ ], is a condition as well.

  • Activated Ability: You can activate this ability by paying the cost before attacking.

  • Automatic Ability When all conditions, this ability is activated automatically.

  • Continuous Ability: This ability activates at the moment you place the card on the field

Types of Charge & Blast Icons

The cost requirement for the abilities are displayed in [ ], Some of the special action icons are listed below.

  • Counter-blast: One type of cost, requiring you to turn the number of cards, as stated next to this icon, in your damage zone from face up to face down.

  • Soul-blast: One type of cost, requiring you to move the number of cards, as stated next to this icon, from the bottom of your vanguard (soul) to the drop zone.

  • Counter-charge: Turn the number of cards, as stated next to this icon, in your damage zone from face down to face up. This enables you to use counter blast.

  • Soul-charge: Move the number of cards, as stated next to this icon, from the top of your deck to the bottom of your vanguard. This enables you to use soul blast.

Example of Reading a Skill

When it is placed on your Vanguard Circle, if you Counter-Blast 1, call up to one "Blaster Blade from your hand or soul to your Rear-Guard Circle, and it gets +10000 power until end of turn. If you called, draw a card."

As a blessing to your vanguard for achieving Grade 3, “Imaginary Gift” is a power bestowed by the fellow units!

When you ride Grade 3 units with the Gift Icon, you can acquire a “Gift Marker”!

“Gift Markers” are separated into three types, namely “Force”, “Accel”, and “Protect”, each with their individual powers. The gift available depends on the clan used!
There are two variations of each marker, you can only pick one variaton of marker per game.
Imaginary Gift: Force

As long as the "Force" gift marker remains on field, the influence of "Force" continues, so units called later on will get the a power boost or change of damage.

Force 1 gives the unit on that circle +10000 on your turn, which can stack if you place multiple of them on the same circle.

Force 2 gives the unit a change on their base critical to 2 for increasing damage, but this does not stack on the same circle.

Imaginary Gift: Accel

When you ride a grade 3 unit with the "Accel" gift icon, you can put an "Accel" gift marker as an additional rear-guard circle in your front row, and call units onto it. Units on these additional circles gain power during your turn.
However, as there is no circle in the back row, beware of the inability to boost. If you ride a unit with "Accel" again, you can put an additional "Accel" gift marker! These markers are placed from left to right and repeats as many times you as you continue gathering accel circles.

Accel 1 grants an additional front row rear-guard circle and during your turn, the unit gains +10000.

Accel 2 grants an additional front row rear-guard circle and during your turn, the unit gains +5000, but you draw 1 card after obtaining it.

Imaginary Gift: Protect

When you ride a grade 3 unit with the "Protect" gift icon, you gain two possibilities:

You can add a "Protect" 1 gift marker to your hand, which has the same ability as a "Sentinel". Although the gift marker needs to be revealed, it can be discarded from your hand as well. Guard perfectly with the "Protect" that can negate any attack.

You can place a "Protect" 2 gift marker on your rear-guard circle. The unit on the circle gains +5000 power during either players' turn. When the unit on a "Protect" 2 marker intercepts, it gains +10000 shield.

Order Cards: Normal & Blitz

The New Card Type: “Order” Appears!
You, the leader of your various Units, now have a new option! It’s name is “Order” cards!
Order in this case means a wide variety of orders, such as strategy, spells, and the use of Items.
As the leader of your Units, master “Order”!

Order Cards are a type of cards that can be used once per turn, and are placed in the Drop Zone after being used. In order to play them, the Grade on your card needs to be equal to or less than the Grade of your Vanguard.


Normal Orders can be played during your main phase.

Blitz Orders can be played during your guard step against an opponent's attack.
